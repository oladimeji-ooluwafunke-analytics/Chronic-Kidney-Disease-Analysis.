# Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) patient data. The analysis focuses on CKD prevalence, patient risk factors, clinical and laboratory indicators, and patient risk patterns.

The dashboard is designed to transform clinical data into clear and actionable insights that can support better understanding of CKD patterns and associated risk factors.

## **Project Status:** Work in Progress  
The dashboard is currently being refined and updated with additional analysis, improved visualizations, and validated insights.

## Tools Used

- Power BI
- Power Query
- DAX
- Excel
- Data Cleaning and Transformation
- Data Visualization

## Dataset

The dataset contains **400 patient records** with clinical and laboratory variables related to Chronic Kidney Disease.

Key variables analyzed include:

- Age
- Blood Pressure
- Hypertension
- Diabetes Mellitus
- Hemoglobin
- Serum Creatinine
- Random Blood Glucose
- Sodium
- Potassium
- CKD Status

Missing values and inconsistencies were handled during the data-cleaning process using Power Query.

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Risk Overview

This page provides an overview of the CKD patient population and major associated risk factors.

Key metrics include:

- Total Patients: 400
- CKD Patients: 237
- CKD Rate: 59.3%
- Average Age: 51.48 years
- Average Blood Pressure: 76.47 mmHg
- High Risk Patients: 90

The page also explores:

- CKD prevalence across age groups
- Diabetes and CKD
- Hypertension and CKD status
- Key patient risk insights

### 2. Clinical & Laboratory Analysis

This page compares important clinical and laboratory indicators between CKD and non-CKD patients.

The analysis includes:

- Average Hemoglobin
- Average Serum Creatinine
- Average Blood Glucose
- Average Sodium
- Average Potassium

Visual comparisons are used to examine how these clinical indicators differ according to CKD status.

### 3. Patient Risk & Trend Analysis

This page focuses on analytical patient risk classification and clinical patterns.

Current risk distribution:

- High Risk: 90 patients
- Moderate Risk: 143 patients
- Low Risk: 167 patients
- High Risk Percentage: 22.5%

The page also analyzes:

- Patient distribution by kidney risk level
- Average patient age by risk level
- Average serum creatinine across age groups and risk levels

> **Note:** The Low, Moderate, and High Risk categories used in this dashboard are analytical classifications created for this project. They should not be interpreted as official clinical CKD stages.

## Key Insights

- CKD prevalence is higher among older patients, particularly those aged 60 and above.
- Diabetes and hypertension are important factors considered in the analysis of CKD patients.
- CKD patients tend to show lower average hemoglobin levels.
- Higher serum creatinine levels are observed among CKD patients in the dataset.
- Clinical and laboratory indicators can help identify patterns associated with kidney health and patient risk.
